About me

Hey there!
I'm Elias, a technical physics master's student doing research in electron structure theory. I'm based in Graz, Austria, where I completed my bachelor's in both Physics and Software Engineering and Management (SEM). I'm passionate about solving complex problems in my studies and work, leveraging the power of modern computing. Outside academics, I balance my screentime by running marathons and, true to my Austrian roots, skiing in the winter.

Education

In the autumn of 2020, I enrolled in Karl-Franzens University Graz for a bachelor's degree in Physics and at Graz University of Technology for a bachelor's in Software Engineering and Management (SEM). After completing both degrees, I continued at TU Graz to pursue a master's in Technical Physics with an exchange semester at TU Delft. Currently, I'm working on my master thesis in the field of machine learning based initialization improvements for faster convergence of Hartree Fock methods within the Hauser Group.

During my undergraduate studies, I explored the complex dynamics of cellular automata, focusing on modeling densest circle packing in two dimensions. This led to intriguing findings, such as hexagonal structures and Rayleigh breakup-like events. For a deeper dive, feel free to check out my bachelor's thesis.
